Benefits of Lunges:

Lunges are excellent lower body exercises that target the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves. They help in building leg strength, improving balance, and enhancing flexibility.

About Lunges:

Lunges are dynamic exercises that involve stepping forward or backward with one leg and lowering the body until both knees are bent at a 90-degree angle. They can be performed with body weight or holding weights for added resistance.

  • Strengthens leg muscles
  • Improves balance and stability
  • Increases hip flexibility
  • Engages core muscles for stability

Lunges offer variations like forward lunges, reverse lunges, or walking lunges, allowing customization based on fitness levels and goals.

How to Perform Lunges:

If you're new to lunges, here's a basic guide to perform them correctly:

  1. Stand tall with feet hip-width apart.
  2. Take a step forward or backward with one leg and lower your body until both knees are bent at 90-degree angles.
  3. Keep your front knee aligned with your ankle and avoid letting it extend over your toes.
  4. Push back to the starting position using the heel of your front foot.
  5. Repeat with the other leg.
