Benefits of Squats:

Squats are a fundamental exercise that targets multiple muscle groups simultaneously. They strengthen the lower body, including the quadriceps, hamstrings, glutes, and calves, while also engaging the core muscles.

About Squats:

Squats are compound exercises that involve bending the knees and lowering the hips, mimicking a sitting position and then standing back up. They can be performed using body weight or with added resistance, such as dumbbells or a barbell.

  • Strengthens leg muscles
  • Tones glutes and thighs
  • Improves overall lower body strength
  • Enhances core stability

Squats are versatile and adaptable to different fitness levels, and they can be performed virtually anywhere without the need for specialized equipment.

How to Do Squats:

If you're new to squats, here's a basic guide to perform them correctly:

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, toes pointing slightly outward.
  2. Keep your back straight, chest up, and engage your core.
  3. Slowly bend your knees and lower your hips as if sitting back into an imaginary chair.
  4. Lower yourself until your thighs are parallel to the ground or as low as comfortable without compromising form.
  5. Push through your heels to return to the starting position, squeezing your glutes at the top.
